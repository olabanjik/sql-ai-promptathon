# Executive Brief

Zava appears to have a concentrated product-quality issue around the Premium Short Sleeve Men’s Top. The strongest signal is not a broad category-wide problem, but a specific premium SKU that is generating meaningful sales while also producing unusually high dissatisfaction, recurring support contacts, and repeated complaints about smart-fabric connectivity after washing. The clearest insight is that this is a product-specific issue affecting B2C customers across multiple markets, and the recommended response is to contain the SKU, investigate the underlying quality problem, and support affected customers proactively.

## Risk cluster
The issue is concentrated in the Premium Short Sleeve Men’s Top, sold as a B2C product through online and retail channels. The complaints are showing up mostly among new customers and span several countries and languages, which suggests this is not isolated to a single market or customer segment.

## Evidence
The SKU generated $19,825.05 in revenue and 228 units sold, while the broader Premium category brought in $190,553.87 and 2,207 units. Support data tells a more concerning story: the SKU had 9 tickets, with an average satisfaction score of 1.67, compared with 2.79 for the Premium category overall. The ticket mix included 1 critical, 2 high, 3 medium, and 3 low-priority cases, and the document evidence was equally consistent, with 6 relevant review documents and 28 relevant support-chat documents. Vector similarity search also clustered the negative documents tightly around the same SKU and the same wash-related connectivity failure pattern.

## Complaint themes
Customers are repeatedly describing a similar experience: the smart fabric stops connecting after washing, the app loses the sensor, and the product does not perform as expected after a few care cycles. Some also raised concerns about unexpected charges and product returns, which suggests that frustration is spilling into billing and post-purchase support as well.

## Recommendation
Zava should treat this as an urgent product-quality and customer-care issue and take immediate containment steps for the Premium Short Sleeve Men’s Top. The first move should be to pause or limit promotion of the SKU while engineering and quality teams investigate the smart-fabric connector and wash durability, and support teams provide a targeted troubleshooting and return playbook for affected customers.

## Confidence and limitations
The conclusion is fairly strong because the evidence lines up across sales, support tickets, chat transcripts, reviews, and vector search. The main limitation is that the analysis is based on a relatively small snapshot of data rather than a longer historical view, so it should be treated as a high-confidence signal rather than a final root-cause determination.
